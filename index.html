Cancer Genomics Analysis Pipeline

+

A comprehensive computational pipeline for analyzing whole-genome and exome sequencing data from cancer samples. Identifies somatic mutations, copy number alterations, and structural variants with high accuracy.

Methods: Variant calling, mutation signature analysis, clonal evolution tracking

Datasets: TCGA, ICGC, in-house patient cohorts (n=2,500)

Impact: Successfully identified novel driver mutations in 15% of analyzed samples

Immunotherapy Response Prediction

+

Machine learning models to predict patient response to immune checkpoint inhibitors using multi-omics data including tumor mutational burden, gene expression profiles, and immune cell infiltration patterns.

Methods: Deep neural networks, ensemble models, SHAP feature importance

Clinical Impact: 78% accuracy in predicting response, improving patient stratification

Collaboration: Multi-center clinical trial with 450+ patients

Single-Cell Cancer Atlas

+

Large-scale single-cell RNA sequencing project mapping cellular heterogeneity in multiple cancer types. Identifies rare cell populations, tumor-immune interactions, and developmental trajectories.

Methods: scRNA-seq, trajectory inference, cell-cell communication analysis, spatial transcriptomics

Scale: 500,000+ cells across 20 cancer types from 300+ patients

Findings: Discovered 12 novel cancer cell states and their role in treatment resistance

Drug Resistance Mechanisms

+

Systematic investigation of molecular mechanisms underlying acquired resistance to targeted therapies. Combines CRISPR screens, drug perturbation experiments, and computational modeling.

Methods: Genome-wide CRISPR screening, proteomics, network analysis, mathematical modeling

Focus: EGFR, ALK, and BRAF inhibitor resistance in lung and melanoma cancers

Results: Identified 25 resistance genes, 8 validated for therapeutic targeting
